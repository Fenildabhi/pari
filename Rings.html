How Much Do Diamond Rings Cost?

The cost of a diamond ring can range dramatically from the low hundreds to hundreds of thousands of dollars. The average amount spent on a diamond engagement ring in the United States is about $5,500, but that price can go up or down depending on a variety of factors. The 4cs of a diamond — carat weight, cut, color and clarity — help determine the value of a diamond ring. The setting also contributes; more ornate designs that are difficult to execute and wide rings that require more precious metal will naturally cost more. Understanding what goes into the price of a diamond ring can help you determine your priorities so you can find a ring that suits your style and budget.

How Do You Buy Diamond Rings?

To buy a diamond ring, start by figuring out what type of ring suits your style. From classic round-cut diamonds to the vintage feel of a cushion-cut stone, each shape projects a different aesthetic. Also consider carat weight, color and clarity — together, those four factors make up the famous “4Cs” used to evaluate and price a loose diamond. Then take a look at some settings. Perhaps you’re more drawn to the feminine lines of a twisted band or maybe it’s all about the simplicity of sleek platinum. Finally, choose a jeweler with a reputation for customer service and high-quality products so you know you’ll love your purchase today, tomorrow and always.

How Much Should An Engagement Ring Cost?

There’s an old saying that dictates engagement ring shoppers should be prepared to spend as much as three months’ salary on their purchase. What many people don’t realize is that “rule” is just a marketing ploy that dates back to the 1930s. In reality, the average amount of money spent on an engagement ring in the United States is $5,500. Some couples spend far less and others dig deep to pay for rings that cost more than a house. Your ring budget is completely up to you. Get to know the factors that affect a ring’s price tag, decide what you’re comfortable spending, and let those parameters guide your shopping experience.
